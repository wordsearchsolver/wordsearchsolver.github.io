Pro Advice

Tips for Better Results

Get the most out of the solver and improve your manual puzzle-solving skills at the same time.

1

Format Your Grid Carefully

Each row on a new line, letters separated by single spaces. Consistent spacing is key — extra spaces or commas will cause parsing errors. Use the sample puzzle to see the correct format.

2

Enter All Words at Once

Don't solve one word at a time. Enter your entire word list in the second box and solve everything in a single click for a complete color-coded answer key.

3

Hover to Focus Words

Once results appear, hover your mouse over any word result in the list to dim all other cells and focus exclusively on that word's path through the grid.

4

Check "Not Found" Words

If a word appears in the "Not Found" list, double-check your grid input for typos. A single wrong letter will cause a mismatch between the grid and the word list.

5

Use Copy to Document Results

Hit the "Copy Results" button to get a plain-text list of all word coordinates. Paste this into a document, email, or study guide for reference.

6

Manual Tip: Start with Rare Letters

When solving manually, start by scanning for rare letters like Q, Z, X, or J rather than scanning for common letters like E or A — it drastically narrows your search area.

7

Manual Tip: Scan Diagonals Last

Horizontal and vertical words are easier to spot. Solve those first, then switch focus to diagonals. Tilting your head or the paper can make diagonal words pop out visually.

8

Load Sample to Learn the Format

Not sure how to format your grid? Click "Load Sample" to see a perfectly formatted 10×10 animals puzzle. You can study the format and then replace it with your own grid.

Background

What Is a Word Search Puzzle?

A word search puzzle (also called a word find, word seek, or word sleuth) is a word game where letters are arranged in a rectangular grid. A list of hidden words accompanies the grid, and the goal is to locate each word within the jumble of letters.

Words can be hidden horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, and may read forwards or backwards. The remaining letter spaces are filled with random letters to disguise the hidden words.

Word searches have been a staple of educational and recreational puzzles since the late 1960s. They are used in classrooms to reinforce vocabulary, in therapy to improve cognitive engagement, and in newspapers and magazines as casual entertainment.

Our Word Search Solver automates the manual scanning process using a precise algorithmic search, scanning every cell in every direction to guarantee complete accuracy.
