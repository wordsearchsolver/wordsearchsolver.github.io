The Most Flexible Free Word Search Generator

What Makes Our Generator Different

Most word search generators place words randomly and fill remaining cells with generic letters. Our generator uses a constraint-satisfaction placement algorithm that maximizes word density while ensuring every word is always findable without overlapping ambiguity.


The difficulty setting controls which directions are used and how "deceptively" filler letters are chosen. On Hard mode, filler letters are selected from the starting letters of your words to create convincing false leads.

FAQ

Word Search Generator — Frequently Asked Questions

How many words can I add to the generator?+

You can add up to 50 words per puzzle. For a 15×15 grid, we recommend 10–20 words. Larger grids (20×20 or 25×25) support more words without crowding. Words that can't fit are listed in the "not placed" section of the word placements panel.

Can I print the puzzle without an answer key?+

Yes! Click the "🖨 Print" button to print the puzzle only — with all hidden words and a word list at the bottom, but without any answer highlighting. Use "🗝 + Key" to print a second version with answers highlighted for use as a teacher's answer key.

What happens if a word doesn't fit in the grid?+

The generator will attempt to place each word up to 200 times in different positions and directions. If a word still can't fit, it's listed as "not placed" in the placements panel. Try increasing the grid size or reducing the number of long words.

Does the word search generator work on mobile?+

Yes. The generator is fully mobile-responsive. On smaller screens, the grid scrolls horizontally. Printing works via your mobile browser's print function — use "Share → Print" on iOS or the browser menu on Android.

Can I save the puzzle to use again later?+

Currently, you can print or use your browser's "Save as PDF" option to store the puzzle. Re-generating with the same word list creates a different layout each time. We recommend saving the PDF immediately after generating your preferred layout.
